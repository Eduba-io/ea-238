01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

About 60 percent of what a team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built.

Applied to a seed-stage decision-intelligence company with a services-heavy deployment model, the map resolves quickly. The ingestion checklist, taxonomy template, role-schema library, and engagement-history archive are database and code work. Data-sensitivity gates, classification markings, role-to-capability mapping rules, and explainability-threshold rules are rule-based logic. Cross-customer taxonomy pattern matching, explanation generation for graph recommendations, and mission-context inference from unstructured data are where AI earns its keep.

Sorting those three before the services team scales is the first conversation.
